## Brief Biography

Dr. Durga Prasad Khatua is a Postdoctoral Research Scholar in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), USA. His research focuses on ultrafast science, nonlinear and high-intensity light–matter interactions, plasma diagnostics, and turbulence studies in magnetically confined fusion plasmas. He is currently involved in the development of mm-wave Doppler backscattering (DBS) diagnostic systems for turbulence and flow measurements in spherical tokamaks, contributing to MAST-U experiments aimed at understanding plasma instabilities and ion cyclotron emission (ICE).

Dr. Khatua received his Ph.D. in Physics from the Homi Bhabha National Institute (HBNI) at the Raja Ramanna Centre for Advanced Technology (RRCAT), Indore, India, in 2022. His doctoral research focused on ultrafast photoexcited carrier dynamics in two-dimensional MoS$_2$, investigating many-body interactions and carrier relaxation processes in monolayer and multilayer systems using femtosecond pump–probe spectroscopy. He also holds both Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees in Physics from Utkal University, Bhubaneswar, India.

His expertise includes ultrafast spectroscopy, femtosecond laser systems, pulse characterization, nonlinear optics, photonic instrumentation, and plasma diagnostic development. Over the course of his research career, Dr. Khatua has designed and developed multiple custom experimental platforms, including ultrafast pump–probe systems, micro-PL/micro-absorption setups, high-field laser interaction experiments, and advanced mm-wave RF diagnostic systems for fusion plasma applications.

## Future Goals

Dr. Khatua’s future research aims to unify **ultrafast science, quantum technologies, and plasma diagnostics** by:  

- Enhancing mm-wave DBS systems for **high-resolution turbulence and flow studies** in fusion plasmas.  
- Investigating **plasma instabilities and ICE phenomena** in spherical tokamaks such as MAST-U.  
- Advancing ultrafast optical techniques, including **attosecond and femtosecond dynamics** in 2D and topological materials, for **quantum information and energy harvesting**.  
- Developing cutting-edge imaging and transient spectroscopy methods (**hyperspectral imaging, ultrafast microscopy**) for **materials and biomedical sciences**.  
- Pursuing compact **laser–plasma accelerators** and platforms that bridge **fusion energy** and **photonic instrumentation**.  

Through these directions, Dr. Khatua aims to contribute to the advancement of **next-generation fusion research** and the broader field of **ultrafast quantum technologies**.
